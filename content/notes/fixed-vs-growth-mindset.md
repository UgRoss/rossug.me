Two ways of thinking about abilities and learning.

## Fixed Mindset

Believes abilities are static — you either have talent or you don't.

**Behaviors:**

- Avoids challenges (fear of failure)
- Hides work from others (avoids feedback)
- Sees effort as pointless if you're not "naturally good"
- Takes criticism personally
- Feels threatened by others' success

## Growth Mindset

Believes abilities can be developed through effort and learning.

**Behaviors:**

- Embraces challenges as learning opportunities
- Seeks feedback and collaboration
- Sees effort as the path to mastery
- Treats criticism as useful information
- Inspired by others' success

## For Developers

| Fixed                   | Growth                                 |
| ----------------------- | -------------------------------------- |
| "I'm not a math person" | "I can learn algorithms with practice" |
| Avoids code reviews     | Welcomes code reviews as learning      |
| Gives up on hard bugs   | Persists and asks for help             |
| Sticks to familiar tech | Explores new languages/tools           |

## Key Insight

The growth mindset isn't about blind optimism — it's about believing that **effort and strategy** lead to improvement.
